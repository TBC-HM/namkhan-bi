Guests who book via Booking.com or Expedia do not give us their real email. We can only reach them through a **relay address** (like `…@guest.booking.com`) that the OTA controls and filters aggressively.

## The rules for OTA traveller emails
- Plain text only — no photos, no formatting, no emojis.
- **No links of any kind.**
- No prices, no marketing words, no CAPITALS.
- Under 180 words, short warm sentences, real signature with the hotel address.
- They receive ONLY: booking confirmation, pre-arrival practicals, and a thank-you. **Never newsletters.** (Timings for these are in [Which emails go out automatically around a booking?](/university/newsletter/newsletter-lifecycle-emails))

:::warning Why this is deliverability-critical
The OTA filter silently drops anything that looks like marketing or tries to pull the guest off-platform. One link or one promotional phrase can get the hotel's messages to **all** OTA guests junked — not just that one email. These rules are the difference between guests receiving their arrival information or nothing at all.
:::

:::never What staff must NEVER change
- Never add a link, photo or offer to an OTA traveller email.
- Never move an OTA guest into a newsletter group — the routing keeps relay addresses in the ota-traveller group on purpose, and broadcasts to them are blocked by policy.
- If an OTA email feels "too plain", that is by design. Do not dress it up.

If a change seems genuinely needed, it goes through PBS with a spam test — never directly.
:::

Expected outcome: you can explain to a colleague why the Booking.com confirmation looks so simple, and you know not to "improve" it.
